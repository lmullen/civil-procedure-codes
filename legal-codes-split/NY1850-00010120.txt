In other actions, costs may be allowed or not, and if allowed, may be apportioned between the parties, on the same or adverse sides, in the discretion of the court. When there are several defendants, not united in interest, and making separate defenses by separate answers, and the plaintifff fails to recover judgment against all, the court may award costs to such of the defendants as have judgment in their favor, or any of them. And in the following cases the costs of an appeal are in the discretion of the court; 1. Where a new trial is ordered: 2. Where a judgment is modified.
