The county treasurer must keep books of entry for money received, invested, or paid out, in legal proceedings, separate from those in which entries are made of county funds, and these books must be delivered: to his successor in office. He must also, at each annual meeting of the board of supervisors, report a statement of the bonds, mortgages and other securities in his hands, and of monies paid; to and deposited or invested by him as provided in this chapter or by special statutes.
