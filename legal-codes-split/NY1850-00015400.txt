To render an appeal effectual for any purpose, a written undertaking must be executed, on the part of the appellant, by at least two sureties, to the effect that the appellant will pay all costs and damage that may be awarded against him on the appeal, not exceeding two hundred and fifty dollars, or that sum must be deposited with the clerk, with whom the judgment or order appealed from was entered, to abide the event of the appeal. The undertaking must be filed with the clerk.
