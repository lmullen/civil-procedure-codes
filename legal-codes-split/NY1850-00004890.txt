The county jail is kept by the sheriff, and is, (except in the city and county of New-York,) used as a prison, 1. For the detention of persons committed in default of security to appear as witnesses in a criminal action, as provided in the code of criminal procedure: 2. For the detention of persons committed for trial for a public offense: 3. For the confinement of persons committed for a contempt, or upon civil process: and 4. For the confinement of persons sentenced to imprisonment therein, upon conviction for a public offense.
