The writ of deliverance must command the defendant to produce the person imprisoned or restrained, by whatsoever name he may be called or charged, and certify and return therewith the time and cause of his imprisonment or restraint, before the supreme court, at a special term to be held at a specified time and place, or before the judge allowing the writ, or another judge as provided in the next section, at a specified, time and place, or immediately after the receipt of the, writ, to do and receive what shall then and there be considered concerning the person so imprisoned or restrained; Or when, for the reason hereinafter mentioned, the body need not be produced, the writ, instead of requiring the production of the person, may require the defendant to certify and return, the time and cause of the imprisonment or restraint.
