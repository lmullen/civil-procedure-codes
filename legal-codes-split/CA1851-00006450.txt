It shall be the duty of every justice, upon the expiration of
his term of office, to deposit with his successor his odicial dockets, as
well his own as those of his predecessors, which may be in his custody,
to be kept as public records. If the office of a Justice become vacant by
his death, or removal from the township or city, or otherwise, before his
successor is elected and qualified, the dockets in possession of such dustice shall be deposited with the County Clerk of the county, to be by
him delivered to the successor in office of the Justice.
