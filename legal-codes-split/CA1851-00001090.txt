Upon the receipt of the affidavit and order, with a written
undertaking, executed by'two or more sufficient sureties, approved by the
Sherin', to the effect that they are bound in double the value of the property as stated in the affidavit, for the prosecution of the action for the
return of the property to the defendant, if return thereof be adjudged, and
for. the payment to him of such sum as may, for any cause, be recovered
against the plaintid', the Sheriff shall forthwith take the property described
in the affidavit, if it be in the possession of the defendant or his agent,
and retain it in his custody. He shall also, without delay, serve on the
defendant a copy of the affidavit, order, and undertaking, by delivering
the same to him personally, if he can be found, or to his agent, from
whose possession the property is taken; or if neither can be found, by
leaving them at the usual place of abode of either, with some person of
suitable age and discretion ; or if neither have any known place of abode,
by putting them into the nearest Post-oilice directed to the defendant.
