The party producing a writing as genuine, which has been
altered, or appears to have been altered, after its execution, in ‘a part material to the question in dispute, and such alteration is not noted on the writing, shall account for the appearance“ or alteration. He may show that the
alteration was made by another, without his concurrence, or was made with
the consent of the parties affected by it, or otherwise properly or innocently
made. If he do that, he may give the writing in evidence, but not otherWISE.
