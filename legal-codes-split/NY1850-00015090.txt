Whenever, from the sickness or infirmity of the party, he cannot, without danger, be produced, the person in whose custody he is, may state that fact in his return to the writ, verifying the same by his oath; and if satisfied of the truth of the allegation, and the return be otherwise sufficient, the court or judge must proceed to decide on the return, and to dispose of the matter in the same manner, as if the party had been produced.
